Supreme Court 5-Judge Bench Constitutional Law April 17, 2026
PENDING PDF Writ Petition (C) 1104/2025

State of Uttarakhand & Ors. v. Union of India & Ors.

The Constitution Bench, by 4:1 majority, held that Parliament's power under Article 3 to alter state boundaries is subject to an implied constitutional duty to meaningfully consult the affected State Legislature, though its consent is not mandatory. The dissenting opinion by Justice Rao held that "meaningful consultation" without binding force renders the requirement nugatory and violates the federal compact. The majority applied the doctrine of constitutional morality and read a procedural obligation into Article 3's requirement of "express views."

Bench: CJI Chandrachud, Justices Oka, Agarwal, Trivedi (majority); Justice S. Rao (dissent)
Article 3 Federalism Constitutional Morality
Delhi HC Fundamental Rights April 16, 2026
W.P.(C) 8821/2025

Priya Sharma v. State of Delhi & Anr.

The High Court directed the Delhi Police to pay ₹5 lakh compensation to a woman subjected to unlawful custodial detention for 11 days without producing before a magistrate, applying the constitutional tort doctrine and holding the State vicariously liable for violations of Articles 21 and 22 by its officers, regardless of good faith.

Bench: Justice Rekha Palli (Single Judge)
Article 21Custodial RightsConstitutional Tort
NGT Environmental Law April 15, 2026
O.A. No. 217/2026

Green Advocates Forum v. Ministry of Environment, Forest & Climate Change

The NGT's Principal Bench imposed environmental compensation of ₹50 crore on a private conglomerate for illegally diverting 340 hectares of Aravalli forest land without prior approval. Invoking the polluter pays and precautionary principles, the Tribunal mandated a restoration plan within 90 days and appointed a court-commissioned oversight committee.

Bench: Chairperson Justice A.K. Goel & Expert Member Dr. A. Senthil Vel
Forest ActPolluter PaysAravalli
Bombay HC Competition Law April 14, 2026
W.P. 1204/2025

Hindustan Unilever Ltd. v. Competition Commission of India

The Division Bench upheld CCI's jurisdiction to investigate anti-competitive practices in FMCG distribution chains even for purely vertical agreements, rejecting arguments that such arrangements cannot attract Section 3(4) of the Competition Act. The Bench dismissed the challenge to the dawn raid search and seizure operation, holding that CCI's investigative powers at that stage are quasi-administrative in nature and subject to limited judicial review.

Bench: Chief Justice D.K. Upadhyaya & Justice Arif Doctor
Section 3(4)CCI JurisdictionFMCG
Supreme Court Tax Law April 17, 2026
Civil Appeal No. 4512/2026

Vikram Enterprises v. Union of India & Ors.

A two-judge bench held that mandatory pre-deposit of 10% of the disputed tax demand as a condition for filing an appeal under the GST Act does not violate Articles 14 or 19(1)(g) of the Constitution. The Court applied the classification test and upheld Parliament's legislative competence, rejecting the argument that the pre-deposit requirement imposes an unreasonable condition on the right to appeal.

Bench: Justices Oka & Agarwal
GST ActPre-DepositArticle 14
Kerala HC Family Law April 14, 2026
Mat. Appeal 442/2025

Anand Krishnan v. Devika R. & Anr.

The Kerala High Court set aside a Family Court order refusing mutual consent divorce citing "short marriage duration," holding that courts cannot substitute their moral judgment for the autonomous decision of two consenting adults. The judgment invokes Article 21's protection of the right to personal liberty and dignity, and holds that prolonging a failed marriage against the wishes of both parties constitutes a violation of fundamental freedoms.

Bench: Justice P.G. Ajithkumar (Single Judge)
